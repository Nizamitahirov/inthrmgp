Core Values

Destination: Riyadh, Saudi Arabia

Growing Tech Hub

Riyadh is rapidly emerging as a technology hub, attracting innovation and talent under Vision 2030.

Economic Landscape

Diversification away from oil. Strong government incentives for foreign investment and non-oil growth.

Legal Framework

Saudization (Nitaqat): Mandatory quotas for hiring nationals. Compliance offers benefits; non-compliance restricts services.

Talent & Infra

Heavy investment in STEM education. World-class digital infrastructure and smart city projects.

Cultural Compass: AZ vs SA

Power Distance (High)

Both are high (AZ: 85, SA: 72). Hierarchy and respect for authority are crucial. Decision-making is centralized.

Uncertainty Avoidance

Azerbaijan (88) and Saudi (64) both prefer structure, rules, and clear instructions. Ambiguity causes stress.

Individualism

AZ is low (28), SA is medium (48). Both value relationships and group loyalty, but SA emphasizes family/tribal bonds more.

Workforce Planning & Staffing

A hybrid staffing approach to balance control and local knowledge.

PCN (Parent Country)

  • Roles: CEO, CFO, Strategic Leads.
  • Why: To ensure alignment with CaspianTech core values and maintain control during startup phase.

TCN (Third Country)

  • Roles: Technical Leads, Project Managers.
  • Why: Sourcing specialized skills from global pools (e.g., Europe) to bridge technical gaps without cultural bias.

HCN (Host Country)

  • Roles: HR, Legal, Sales Managers.
  • Why: Critical for navigating local labor laws (Nitaqat), building client relationships, and cultural insight.

Selection & Development

Expat Selection Criteria

  • Technical Competence & Proven Expertise.
  • Cross-Cultural Aptitude & Emotional Intelligence.
  • Islamic Cultural Sensitivity.
  • Family Adaptability (Crucial factor).

Pre-Departure Training

  • Cultural Assimilation (Islamic norms).
  • Basic Arabic Language skills.
  • Understanding Saudization laws.
  • Logistical Support (Housing in Compounds).

On-Going & Repatriation

  • Mentorship (HQ Leader + Local Mentor).
  • Continuous cultural coaching.
  • Reverse Culture Shock preparation.
  • Clear career path upon return.

Performance Management System

Fair & Culturally Sensitive: Combining Global Standards with Local Context.

Hybrid Approach

Standard metrics (code quality, completion) combined with locally adapted behavioral competencies (respect for hierarchy).

SMART Goals

Co-created goals emphasizing both individual and team contributions, respecting authority structures.

Feedback Style

Continuous coaching. Feedback must balance directness with sensitivity (Face-saving) appropriate for Saudi culture.

360-Degree

Adapted for high power distance context. Peer recognition to foster supportive work environment.

Expatriate Package

  • Base Salary: Adjusted for Saudi market purchasing power.
  • Premium: 15-25% Foreign Service Premium (Hardship/Inducement).
  • Allowances: COLA (Cost of Living), Housing (Compounds), Education.
  • Relocation: Full support for moving and repatriation.
  • Healthcare: Comprehensive cover including Medical Evacuation.
  • Banking: Consideration of Sharia-compliant financial arrangements.

Host Country Employee (HCN)

  • Base Salary: Competitive & aligned with local market benchmarks.
  • Bonuses: Performance-based (Individual & Team KPIs).
  • Benefits: Comprehensive Health Insurance & Paid Time Off.
  • Development: Training access, certifications, & career paths.
  • Equity: Stock options to align interest with company success.
  • GOSI: Full compliance with Social Insurance contributions.

Mandatory GOSI

Social insurance contributions for both employer and employee.

Health Insurance

Mandatory national health insurance coverage for all workers.

Labor Regulations

Strict adherence to hours (48h/week), overtime (150%), and EOSB.
